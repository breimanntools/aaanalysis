.. _protocols:

Protocols
=========

A *tutorial* teaches you one function; a **protocol** teaches you a **workflow**.
Each protocol answers a single biological question end-to-end — *when to use it,
what goes in, the minimal code, what comes out, how to interpret it, common
mistakes,* and *what to do next* — and, above all, builds the mental model for
**when and why** to reach for each tool.

.. figure:: /_static/img/protocols_gallery.png
   :align: center
   :width: 100%
   :alt: Gallery of AAanalysis protocol outputs

   The Protocols catalog at a glance — each tile is the headline figure of one
   protocol, from exploratory sequence analysis through validating a result.

**The mental model.** AAanalysis turns a biological *question* into an
*interpretable comparison*. You bring two (or more) groups of sequences; CPP reads
out the **signature** — the physicochemical features, resolved by position, that
distinguish them — and the rest of the pipeline helps you sample fairly, engineer
features, select what matters, predict, explain, and check that the signal is
real. The catalog below follows that data flow: it opens with the two canonical
protocols (the CPP signature and the prediction levels), then walks the pipeline
from constructing sets through to validating your result.
